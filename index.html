Albatross

Albatross, a very large white seabird of a family (Diomedeidae) of large, web-footed tubenose birds found chiefly in the South Seas. They have long, narrow wings and a long, hooked beak

Fun Facts

  • An albatross has the largest wingspan of any living bird
  • They can go years without touching land
  • They can live and raise chicks into their 60s
  • They mate for life, with some wiggle room
  • They court each other with elaborate mating dances
  • They can smell food in the water from 12 miles away

Did you know?

  • Scientific Name:Diomedeidae
  • Nick Name:Mollymawk
  • Wing Span: 12 Feet (3.7m)
  • Average Lifespan: 50 Years
  • Habitat: Southern Ocean and the North Pacific
